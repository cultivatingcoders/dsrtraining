Pathway to Quality Services Developing and enhancing employee knowledge, skills and abilities.

    PURPOSE:
    Department for Self Reliance PATHWAY TO QUALITY SERVICES establishes a standard and consistent process for developing and enhancing the knowledge, skills, and abilities of the Department for Self Reliance (DSR) to attain and maintain proficiency in areas related to their duties and responsibilities.
    DSR's Mission:
    "We shall serve, with integrity and ethics, to empower individuals and families by promoting T'aa hw6 ajft 'eego through appropriate support, opportunities and education". In order for DSR to meet the Mission and provide quality Customer services, the DSR must provide a learning environment that offers a range of courses for employees to increase their knowledge, skills, and abilities so that they are proficient in performing the duties and responsibilities associated with their position.
    Our Values:
      Knowledge - With facts, information, and skills acquired by a person through experience or education.
      Skills - With the ability to do something well; expertise.
      Abilities - With talent, skill, or proficiency in a particular area.
    DSR's Goal:
    To invest in every employee by offering an opportunity of training allowing them to become experts in their line of work. According to the DSR's Tribal Family Assistance Plan (TFAP), the following Assurances is our commitment to provide professional growth and ensure customers receive efficient and effective services:
    • Fiscal Accountability: The Nation will comply with the fiscal accountability provisions of Section 5(t)(1) of the Indian Self-Determination and Education Assistance Act (25 USC 450c (t)(l), relating to the submission of a single-agency audit report required by Chapter 75 of Title 31, United States Code. Other internal controls were established within the DSR for enhanced fiscal accountability. Administrative functions to prevent, identify, and deter fraud; recover and reconcile improper payments to Customers; and to execute quality assurance were established within the DSR.
    • Dispute Resolution Process: A Customer who is not satisfied with a decision made by the DSR will be afforded an opportunity to dispute the decision, pursuant to the procedures set forth in the DSR Doing Procedures Manual. The dispute resolution process affords the Customer a fair hearing that meets the standards comparable to the provisions in the Tribal TANF Program Final Rules 45 CFR 286.75(a)(7).
    • Protection of Customer Confidentiality: Pursuant to the Navajo Nation Privacy Act (CAP-48-99), the DSR Professional and Ethical Standards of Employee Conduct, the DSR Pathway to Self Reliance Manual; the DSR assures it will uphold and protect the confidentiality of DSR Customers.
    • Equitable Assistance and Services: Eligible families moving into the DSR Service Delivery Area will receive the same amount of cash assistance and/or applicable non-cash assistance and supportive services as all other eligible individuals and families.
    • Prevention of Duplication Assistance: Staff training will regularly be provided in areas of interviewing, skills, completion of application, potential fraud detection, and DSR Policies and Procedures relative to prevention of duplication assistance.
